Calendar Subscription

Subscribe to keep your calendar in sync with your race schedule. Events you've RSVP'd Yes or Maybe to will appear automatically, and updates sync whenever your calendar app refreshes.

Subscribe vs. Import: All methods on this page create a live subscription that stays in sync with your race schedule. If you simply download and open an .ics file, it will only import a one-time snapshot that won't update.

Setup Instructions

Easiest way: Tap the "Open in Calendar App" button above. Your iPhone will ask you to confirm the subscription — tap Subscribe and you're done.

Manual setup:

  1. Copy the Calendar URL above.
  2. Open the Settings app on your iPhone or iPad.
  3. Scroll down and tap Calendar (or Mail on older iOS versions).
  4. Tap Accounts > Add Account > Other.
  5. Under Calendars, tap Add Subscribed Calendar.
  6. Paste the URL into the Server field and tap Next.
  7. Review the settings and tap Save.

Creates a live subscription that stays in sync. iOS typically refreshes every few hours.

Easiest way: Click the "Open in Calendar App" button above. Calendar will open and ask you to confirm — click Subscribe.

Manual setup:

  1. Copy the Calendar URL above.
  2. Open the Calendar app on your Mac.
  3. From the menu bar, click File > New Calendar Subscription...
  4. Paste the URL into the Calendar URL field and click Subscribe.
  5. Choose a name, color, and how often to auto-refresh (every hour is recommended).
  6. Click OK.

Creates a live subscription that stays in sync. If you use iCloud, it will sync to all your Apple devices automatically.

Google Calendar requires the HTTPS URL. This must be done from a computer, not the mobile app.

  1. Copy the HTTPS URL above.
  2. Open Google Calendar in a web browser on your computer.
  3. In the left sidebar, find "Other calendars" and click the + icon next to it.
  4. Select From URL.
  5. Paste the subscription URL into the URL of calendar field.
  6. Click Add calendar.

Creates a live subscription that stays in sync. Google Calendar typically refreshes once every 12–24 hours.

This is the easiest way to subscribe with Outlook. The calendar will sync to all your Outlook apps (desktop, mobile, and web) automatically.

  1. Copy the Calendar URL above.
  2. Open Outlook Calendar in your browser (or Office 365 Calendar if you use a work account).
  3. Click Add calendar in the left sidebar.
  4. Select Subscribe from web.
  5. Paste the URL, give the calendar a name, and click Import.

Creates a live subscription that stays in sync. Outlook typically refreshes every 3 hours. The subscription will appear on all your devices signed into the same Microsoft account.

New Outlook:

  1. Copy the Calendar URL above.
  2. Open Outlook and switch to the Calendar view.
  3. Click Add calendar in the left sidebar.
  4. Select Subscribe from web.
  5. Paste the URL, give the calendar a name, and click Import.

Classic Outlook:

  1. Copy the Calendar URL above.
  2. Go to File > Account Settings > Account Settings.
  3. Click the Internet Calendars tab.
  4. Click New... and paste the URL.
  5. Click Add, optionally rename the calendar, then click OK.

Creates a live subscription that stays in sync. Outlook typically refreshes every 3 hours.

Outlook for Mac does not support calendar subscriptions directly. Use Outlook on the Web instead — the subscription will sync to your Mac automatically.

  1. Copy the Calendar URL above.
  2. Open Outlook Calendar in your browser (or Office 365 Calendar for work accounts).
  3. Click Add calendar in the left sidebar.
  4. Select Subscribe from web.
  5. Paste the URL, give the calendar a name, and click Import.

Creates a live subscription that stays in sync. The subscription will appear in your Outlook for Mac app once it syncs with your Microsoft account.

Your calendar app will refresh automatically (typically every few hours, depending on the app). Changes to your RSVPs will appear at the next refresh.
